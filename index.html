About Me

With warmth, thoughtfulness, and a touch of humor, I strive to create a welcoming, judgment-free space where you can feel truly heard and supported. As a former student-athlete at the University of Pennsylvania, I understand the challenges of balancing high expectations with personal well-being. I bring this insight into our work together, while helping clients build confidence, resilience, and a sense of calm. I have experience working with young adults and adults from diverse backgrounds facing anxiety, depression, self-esteem, relationship challenges and career stress. I utilize an integrative approach, helping you work through difficult emotions and situations. I draw from psychodynamic therapy, CBT, IFS, and mindfulness to best support each client’s unique situation. Grounded by time outdoors and meaningful connections with loved ones, I look forward to providing a space where you can grow into the version of yourself that you’re most proud of.

Education & Credentials Bachelor of Arts in Sociology, Minor in Nutrition, University of Pennsylvania Masters in Clinical Mental Health Counseling, Northwestern University Somatic IFS, IFS Institute, 2025 Foundations of the IFS Model, IFS Institute (In Progress) CBT for Anxiety, Beck Institue (In Progress)

Self-Esteem & Perfectionism

Self-esteem is how we see and value ourselves—our sense of worth, confidence, and belonging. When it's low, it can shape how we show up in relationships, work, and daily life. In therapy, we create space to understand where those beliefs come from and gently begin to build a more compassionate, grounded connection to yourself.

Relationship Issues

All relationships face challenges—but when stress builds, it can leave us feeling disconnected, frustrated, or alone. Whether it's communication breakdowns, recurring conflict, or shifting dynamics, relationship stress can take a real toll on our emotional well-being. In therapy, we create a space to untangle what's happening, understand patterns, and explore healthier ways of relating.

Career Stress & Performance

Work can be a major source of stress—whether it’s burnout, pressure to perform, lack of direction, or feeling stuck in a role that no longer fits. In therapy, we create space to explore what’s weighing on you, clarify your values and goals, and find ways to navigate work with more balance, purpose, and self-compassion.

Eating Disorders & Body Image

Body image and eating concerns can feel all-consuming—whether it’s the pressure to look a certain way, a fraught relationship with food, or the inner critic that never quiets. In therapy, we create space to untangle these struggles, understand their roots, and build a more compassionate, empowered connection to your body and self.

Family & Cultural Dynamics

Family and cultural dynamics can be deeply complex—whether it’s navigating expectations, intergenerational tension, feeling caught between identities, or carrying the weight of unspoken roles. In therapy, we create space to explore these layers, honor your experiences, and support you in finding your voice and your path with clarity and care.

Anxiety

Anxiety can show up in so many ways—racing thoughts, constant worry, overthinking, or feeling overwhelmed in social situations. In therapy, we create space to slow down, understand the patterns beneath the anxiety, and build tools to move through life with more ease, confidence, and self-trust.
